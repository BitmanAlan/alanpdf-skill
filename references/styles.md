# AlanPDF Styles

## Purpose

Styles sit on top of blueprints. The blueprint decides the document grammar. The style decides the visual mood.

Use `--style <name>` with either blueprint.

## `navy-consulting`

Use for:

- strategy proposals
- whitepapers
- internal decision memos
- client-facing consulting output

Signals:

- deep navy headings
- classic corporate blue table headers
- cool gray metadata
- neutral white canvas

## `emerald-executive`

Use for:

- executive briefings
- transformation programs
- board or leadership readouts
- documents that should feel “premium” without becoming flashy

Signals:

- teal-green accent
- cool green-tinted callout backgrounds
- darker slate-green body ink

## `charcoal-minimal`

Use for:

- minimalist internal docs
- operating plans
- serious documents where color should recede

Signals:

- charcoal accents instead of bright blue
- cool gray borders
- restrained monochrome feel

## `warm-whitepaper`

Use for:

- research notes
- whitepapers
- longer narrative documents
- external thought-leadership style pieces

Signals:

- warm paper background
- muted brown accent
- softer reading feel than the default consulting palette

## `broker-classic`

Use for:

- securities research reports
- investment-advisory notes
- broker-style equity coverage

Signals:

- strong sell-side blue accent
- pale blue research summary strips
- crisp institutional white background

## `sellside-slate`

Use for:

- serious market notes
- neutral institutional research
- house-view briefings where color should stay subdued

Signals:

- slate-blue headers
- restrained gray-blue tables
- minimal, analyst-desk feel

## `ir-clean`

Use for:

- investor-relations summaries
- management communication packs
- market-facing updates that should feel lighter than classic broker output

Signals:

- clean cyan-teal accent
- airy pale panels
- less heavy than broker-style blue
